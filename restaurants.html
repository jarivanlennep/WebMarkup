Restaurants

Eat breakfast, lunch or dinner at the most special restaurants in the heart of the city. Here are our tips!

Pancake Bakery

Treat your tastebuds in a Dutch culinary classic at the Pancake Bakery. It's the most famous pancake restaurant in the Netherlands and it had guests like Leonardo Dicaprio and Paris Hilton. They have pancakes for anyones taste. Try the authentic dutch Pancake with stroop (syrup) or get one of the 25 international Pancakes with tastes of each country. Inside it's cozy and dark but with dim warm lights making it feel like a special old dutch restaurant. It's also less then 100 meters away from Anne Frank Museum so it's a good stop after the museum visit. They are open for breakfast, lunch and dinner.

For more specific opening hours and their menu, check out their website here:https://pancake.nl

De Silveren Spiegel

Coming from the 17th century, De Silveren Spiegel is one of the oldest restaurant in Amsterdam, so it doesn't get more authentic and traditional than this! This is also the number one place to go for a high quality authentic Dutch culinary experience. The meal gets served in one of the four intimate and characteristic rooms. Eating at De Silveren Spiegel is a garanteed unique and special dining experience.

To reserve, to see the menu or to check opening hours, see their website here:https://silverenspiegel.com/nl/landing-dd

Rainarai

Also located near the Anne Frank House, this Algerian restaurant is a beautiful and special place to eat. You can sit outside looking at one of the most beautiful canal views, or you can sit inside in the charming little restaurant with a candle light view of the canal. All of this while you're enjoying the healthy, ecological and tasty dishes of the Algerian chef. Definitely try his famous pumpkin spinach pie with goat cheese. Don't miss this unique address while staying in Amsterdam!

For the menu and the opening hours:https://www.rainarai.nl/traiteur-prinsengracht
